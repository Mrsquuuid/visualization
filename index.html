Food Visualization in Asia & Oceania









Introduction

In the era of coronavirus, the logistics between countries has received a lot of impact, and the issue of food security has attracted the attention of many countries. Since Asia's population accounts for 53.4% of the world's total, this issue will receive more attention. In addition, due to geographical location, the logistics of many countries in Oceania have also received a great impact, so this website also counts them. What's more, 64 % of starving children in the whole world lives in Asia. We have to face this problem and find ways to improve it. As we all know, as many as 3.5 billion people in the world depend on rice for their livelihoods, and Asians are very dependent on rice.

This website will analyze each food category one by one, especially popular crops such as rice.

Largest Food Producer in Asia


 Rice is the first grain in China. China is the largest rice producer in the world, and its rice production area is second only to India. In addition, China is also a powerful country in rice science and technology in the world, and the world-famous rice hybridization was first applied to production in China. The stable growth of rice in China plays a very important basic role in ensuring the food security of the country and even the world. In China, about 70 % of Chinese people rely on rice. China's rice planting area is about 30 million hectares, and there are 12 provinces with a planting area of more than 667000 hectares. The rice area of these 12 provinces accounts for about 85% of the total rice area in China.

However, several factors have threatened the sustainability and productivity of traditional puddled transplanted rice production system, such as :
  • climate change
  • decline in rice planting area
  • scarcity of labor availability
  • narrow genetic background of rice varieties
  • overuse of fertilizers and chemicals
